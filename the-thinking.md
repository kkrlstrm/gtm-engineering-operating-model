# The thinking behind it

This operating model is distilled from a series of essays on what leadership becomes when a team's real work is directing agents. The repo is the compressed, GitHub-appropriate form; the essays are the full arguments, with the sources and the counterarguments worked through.

They're listed here roughly in the order that builds the model — from *where* the leader stands, to *what* they provision, to *how* judgment survives.

---

### Where the leader stands

**[The Layer Question — where leaders belong when teams run AI](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/layer-question-where-leaders-belong-when-teams-run-ai-kai-karlstrom-qnzae)**
Leadership leverage is a layer up — in intent and the playbook — not in running the agents or doing the work. Pick a layer and stay at it. → [Principle 1](principles.md)

**[The Builder's Trap — why the faster leader should give the build away](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/builders-trap-why-leaders-should-delegate-age-ai-kai-karlstrom-opsje/)**
The build you can do fastest is the one you should hand off; speed at the build buys blindness to the layer you've left. Build to set the pattern, then hand off — and dip back deliberately to stay calibrated. → [Principle 6](principles.md)

### What the leader provisions

**[The Expertise Need Is Dissolving. Protection Is What Leaders Provision Now.](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/expertise-need-dissolving-protection-what-leaders-now-kai-karlstrom-ea5ae/)**
AI commoditized expertise, so the strand a leader provisions flips to protection: shield people from being blamed, replaced, or embarrassed by their agents. The leader as bodyguard for human dignity. → [Principle 2](principles.md)

**[Leadership in the Age of AI Agents (The Leader as Friction)](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/leadership-age-ai-agents-kai-karlstrom-umdfe)**
When every agent confirms you with a clean answer, the leader's job is to be the friction that asks again — and the org reshapes toward small human teams over agent swarms. → [Principle 3](principles.md)

### What survives, and how it transfers

**[Developing Taste — the only leadership skill that survives the agent](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/developing-taste-only-leadership-skill-survives-agent-kai-karlstrom-zywxe)**
Judgment — knowing when the confident answer is wrong — is the durable skill once execution is commoditized. → [Principle 4](principles.md)

**[The Taste Famine — transferring judgment when agents have eaten the reps](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/how-transfer-judgment-when-agents-have-eaten-reps-kai-karlstrom-2vyoe/)**
Taste grew as the residue of doing junior reps badly; agents now do those reps, so the substrate is vanishing. Rebuild it on purpose — predict-then-compare, narrate rejected options, proximity over docs. → [Principle 7](principles.md), [Growing judgment](growing-judgment.md)

### Catching what fails silently

**[Permission for Inarticulate Doubt](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/permission-inarticulate-doubt-kai-karlstrom-5glue)**
Most agent failures are silent. The cheapest detector is a human allowed to say "this looks wrong, I can't say why yet" and stop the run — which only works if you've made that safe and funded the false alarm. → [Principle 5](principles.md), [Failure & doubt](failure-and-doubt.md)
